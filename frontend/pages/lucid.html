Techniques
MILD Mnemonic Induction ›
How it works: Before sleep, vividly imagine yourself becoming lucid in a recent dream. Repeat your intention ("I will know I'm dreaming") as you fall asleep.

Best time: After a natural wake-up, when you've just been in REM sleep. Set an alarm for 5–6 hours after sleep, wake up briefly, then go back to sleep with your intention.

Success tip: The more detailed your mental rehearsal, the stronger the signal to your dreaming brain.
WBTB Wake Back to Bed ›
How it works: Set an alarm for 5–6 hours after you fall asleep. Wake up and stay awake for 20–30 minutes (read about lucid dreaming, do light activity). Then go back to sleep with your intention.

Why it works: You re-enter REM sleep quickly and your mind is primed for awareness — a perfect window for lucidity.

Combine with MILD for significantly higher success rates.
Reality Testing Habit of Questioning ›
How it works: During the day, regularly ask "Am I dreaming?" and actually check — look at your hands, try to push a finger through your palm, read text twice (it changes in dreams).

The goal: Build it into a genuine habit so the same check happens automatically inside a dream.

Best triggers: Every time you walk through a door, check your phone, or feel a strong emotion — ask the question.
WILD Wake-Initiated Lucid Dream ›
How it works: Transition directly from waking consciousness into a dream — maintaining awareness as your body falls asleep. Best attempted after WBTB.

Process: Lie completely still. Focus on the hypnagogic imagery (patterns, sounds) behind your closed eyes. Don't react — just observe, and let yourself slide into the dream.

Advanced: This takes practice but produces the most vivid and controllable lucid dreams.
